QR code payload

The QR value is a Base64 string built from Tag-Length-Value fields encoded from UTF-8 text. Phase 2 uses tags 1 to 9 when applicable; tag 9 applies to simplified invoices and their notes.

Tag Value placed in QR payload
1Seller name
2Seller VAT registration number
3Invoice issue timestamp
4Invoice total including VAT
5VAT total
6Hash of the XML invoice
7ECDSA signature
8ECDSA public key
9ZATCA CA signature of the cryptographic stamp public key, where required

Verification checkpoints

  1. Confirm the transaction is taxable. Non-taxable receipts skip ZATCA QR and clearance.
  2. Confirm seller tax identity, branch, EGS CSID status, private key availability, and active environment.
  3. Confirm buyer routing: standard clearance for B2B tax invoice data, simplified reporting for B2C.
  4. Validate invoice math: line totals, VAT categories, discounts, total including VAT, and currency.
  5. Lock the source charge and reserve ICV plus previous invoice hash atomically before submission.
  6. Build canonical UBL XML, compute hash, sign/stamp, then generate QR from the final signed XML facts.
  7. For standard invoices, wait for ZATCA clearance before exposing the customer document.
  8. For simplified invoices, share after local QR/stamp, then report to ZATCA within 24 hours.
  9. Store XML, QR payload, API response, hash chain state, and audit log. Never delete a generated invoice.
